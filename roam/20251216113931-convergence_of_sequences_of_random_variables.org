* Portmanteau Lemma
** Statement
Let $(M, d)$ be a Metric space, $X_n$ be a seq of rv $M$ valued:
The Portmanteau lemma states that the following assertions are equivalent:
 * For any $\varphi$ continuous and bounded: $\lim_n \Ex[\varphi(X_n)] = \Ex[\varphi(X)]$
 * For any $\varphi$ uniformly continuous and bounded: $\lim_n \Ex[\varphi(X_n)] = \Ex[\varphi(X)]$
 * For any closed set $F$ of $M$: $\limsup_n \Pr[X_n \in F] \leq \Pr[X\in F]$
 * For any open set $O$ of $M$: $\liminf_n \Pr[X_n\in O] \geq \Pr[X\in O]$
 * For any Borel set $A$, with $\Pr[X\in\partial A] = 0$, $\lim_n \Pr[X_n\in A] = \Pr[X\in A]$
** Proof
 * $1 \Rightarrow 2$ trivially
 * A$2\Rightarrow 3$ Let $F$ be a closed set of $M$. We define $F_k=\{x\in M | d(x,F) \leq 1/k\}$
   We define also $\varphi_k(x) = 1-k\cdot d(x,F)$ if $x\in F_k$, 0 elsewhere.
   We have then $\mathbbm{1}_{F} \leq \varphi_k \leq \mathbbm{1}_{F_K}$. The left inequality gives us that $\limsup_n \Pr[X_n \in F] \leq \limsup_n \Ex[\varphi_k(X_n)]$

   Finally, since $f$ and $x\mapsto d(x, F)$ are 1-Lipschitz, $\varphi_k$ is uniformly continuous, and thus
\begin{equation}
\limsup_n \Ex[\varphi_k(X_n)] = \lim_n \Ex[\varphi_k(X_n)] = \Ex[\varphi_k(X)]
\end{equation}
From the right inequality, we have
\begin{equation}
\Ex[\varphi_k(X)] \leq \Pr[X \in F_k]
\end{equation}
thus
\begin{equation}
\limsup_n \Prob[X_n \in F] \leq \Prob[X\in F_k]
\end{equation}
