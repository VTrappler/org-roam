#+title: Langevin Monte Carlo
#+filetags: :Bayesian:

Let $\pi$ be a pdf on $\mathbb{R}^d$. We want to [[id:4c2833a0-5351-4fba-b25e-4985acbd205f][Sampling methods]] iid samples from it.

The Langevin diffusion of a function $f$ twice differentiable is
\begin{equation}
dX_t = - \nabla f(X_t)dt + \sqrt{2}dB_t
\end{equation}

Using Fokker Planck equation, the diffusion process defined is equivalent to
\begin{equation}
\partial_t \mu_t(x) = \nabla [\mu_t(x)\nabla f(x)] + \Delta \mu_t(x)
\end{equation}
where $\mu_t$ is the pdf of $X_t$.

We define $\nu(x) = c e^{-f(x)}$:
$\Delta\nu = -\nabla (\nabla f(x)\nu)^{}$ Hence $\nu$ is the stationary distribution of the SDE
