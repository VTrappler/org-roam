A concentration inequality refers to a bound (in [[id:f16e587c-de26-47f5-8f1b-e298fd7eb3b6][Probability]]) on the deviation of a random variable to some value (typically the  expected value)

* Markov Inequality
Let $X$ be a non negative r.v., then, for every $a>0$,
$P[X\geq a] \leq E[X]/a$
Proof:
Let's consider $a1_{X>a}$, that is $a$ when $X>a$, 0 elsewhere. Since $X$ is positive a.s., $X\geq a1_{X>a}$ a.s, and then
$E[X] \geq E[a1_{X>a}]$ so $E[X] \geq aP[X>a]$
