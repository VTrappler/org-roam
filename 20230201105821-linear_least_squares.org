#+title: Linear Least-Squares
#+startup: latexpreview

Let us consider the [[id:d64056c7-f969-484c-baf5-d5f2726ce4ba][Linear System]] $Ax = b$, $A\in\mathbb{R}^{n \times
p}$, $x \in \mathbb{R}^p$, $y\in \mathbb{R}^n$

If the system is overdetermined: $n > p$,
the system has no solution, thus we can look for $\hat{x} = \text{argmin} \|Ax - b \|^2 = \text{argmin} J(x)$

 * This argmin is the Orthogonal [[id:86b28d90-52e6-4b84-ab83-e3a1c01d717e][Projection]] of $b$ onto the subspace
  generated by the columns of $A$, its [[id:fa41eb46-4983-486d-bcd5-75e26728621f][Pseudoinverse]]
 * $\nabla J = 2A^T(Ax - b)$

We have
\begin{align}
\nabla J = 2A^T(A\hat{x} - b) = 0 \iff A^TA\hat{x} &= A^Tb \\
\hat{x} &= (A^TA)^{-1}A^Tb \\
\hat{x} &= A^+b
\end{align}
